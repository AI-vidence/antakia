Ai-act

The AI Act classifies AI systems based on their risk levels, with corresponding regulations for providers and users. Notably, the legislation emphasizes the importance of safety, transparency, traceability, non-discrimination, and environmental respect in AI systems used within the EU.

Key elements of the legislation include:

Risk Classification:

1. Unacceptable Risk:

  • Systems posing an unacceptable risk, considered a threat to individuals, are outright prohibited. Examples include cognitive-behavioral manipulation of vulnerable groups and real-time remote biometric identification like facial recognition.

2. High Risk:

  • AI systems with a negative impact on security or fundamental rights fall into this category, further divided into two groups:
    • Systems used in EU-regulated product categories (e.g., toys, aviation, medical devices).
    • Systems in eight specific domains requiring registration in a EU database, including biometric identification, critical infrastructure management, education, employment, law enforcement, migration control, and legal interpretation.

Generative AI:

  • Generative AI, such as ChatGPT, must adhere to transparency requirements, indicating AI-generated content, preventing the generation of illegal content, and publishing summaries of copyrighted training data.

Limited Risk:

  • AI systems with limited risk must meet minimal transparency requirements to enable users to make informed decisions. Users interacting with AI-generated content (e.g., deepfakes) should be informed, allowing them to decide whether to continue use.
